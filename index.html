Addis Ababa City Tour — Details

A day in the capital that blends history, coffee rituals, and modern energy.

Itinerary highlights

  • National Museum: Encounter Lucy and Ethiopia’s deep past.
  • Mercato: One of Africa’s largest open-air markets, vibrant and layered.
  • Coffee ceremony: Taste heritage, aroma, and community in a cup.
  • Mount Entoto views: Panoramic cityscapes and cooler air.

Inclusions

  • Licensed local guide
  • Private transport
  • Entrance fees
  • Coffee ceremony session

Lalibela & Simien Peaks — Details

A journey between sacred architecture and raw mountain drama.

Lalibela highlights

  • Rock-hewn churches (Bet Giyorgis, Bet Medhane Alem)
  • Pilgrimage atmosphere and local hymns
  • Traditional cuisine and crafts

Simien highlights

  • Gelada baboons and endemic fauna
  • Escarpments, ridges, and sunrise viewpoints
  • Ranger-guided safety and route planning

Frequently asked questions

Answers to the most common queries before you book.

Is it safe to travel?

Safety is our priority. We use vetted transport, experienced guides, and clear briefings. We also tailor routes based on current conditions.

Can you customize itineraries?

Yes. Whether you want more culture, hiking, photography, or family-friendly pacing, we’ll adapt the plan to fit your interests and time.

What’s included in pricing?

Typically: guide, transport, entrance fees, and some activities. We provide clear inclusions and optional add-ons up front — no hidden fees.

Do you arrange accommodations?

We can recommend and book lodges and hotels, with community-run and eco-friendly options where possible.

Sustainability practices

Travel that respects ecosystems and cultures.

  • Partnering with local communities and fair compensation
  • Choosing eco-friendly accommodations and transport when possible
  • Respecting wildlife and protected habitats
  • Minimizing waste and encouraging refillable water bottles

Photography guidance

Capture memories respectfully and beautifully.

Etiquette

Ask before photographing people, follow local guidance, and share portraits when appropriate.

Best light

Golden hours — shortly after sunrise and before sunset — provide the most flattering and cinematic light.
